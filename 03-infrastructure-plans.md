## 1. Dubai 2040 Urban Master Plan

The Dubai 2040 Urban Master Plan, launched by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum in March 2021, is the long-term strategic framework for the emirate's urban development.

### Key Objectives:
- Double green and recreational areas
- Increase beach length from 21 km to 105 km
- Five main urban centers: **Deira-Bur Dubai**, **Downtown-Business Bay**, **Dubai Marina-JBR**, **Expo City/Dubai South**, and **Silicon Oasis-Academic City**
- Three major economic zones per center
- Population target: **7.8 million by 2040** (from ~3.5M in 2023)

## 6. Government Housing Regulations & Rent Cap Rules

### RERA Rent Index & Rent Cap:
- **RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority)** controls rent increases
- Landlords can only increase rent based on the **RERA Rental Index**
- The system works on a tier basis:

| Current Rent vs. Market Rate | Maximum Increase Allowed |
|---|---|
| Up to 10% below market | 0% (no increase) |
| 11-20% below market | 5% |
| 21-30% below market | 10% |
| 31-40% below market | 15% |
| 40%+ below market | 20% |

### Key Regulations:
- **Ejari registration:** Mandatory for all lease contracts (registered with DLD)
- **12-month notice:** Required for any eviction or major changes
- **Service charges:** Regulated by RERA; must be disclosed
- **Security deposit:** Typically 5% of annual rent (unfurnished), 10% (furnished)
- **Rent payment:** Typically 1-4 cheques per year (landlord preference)
- **Short-term rentals:** Regulated by Dubai Tourism (DTCM) â€” requires holiday home license

### Impact on Investors:
- Rent cap protects against market downturns (tenants stay longer)
- But also limits upside in rapidly appreciating areas
- Short-term rental licensing adds complexity but can boost yields by 20-40%

Source: [RERA](https://dubailand.gov.ae), [Dubai REST App](https://dubailand.gov.ae)
