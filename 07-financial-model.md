## 7. Tax Implications

### Dubai (UAE)
- ‚úÖ **Zero income tax** ‚Äî all rental income is tax-free in UAE
- ‚úÖ **Zero capital gains tax** ‚Äî property sales are tax-free
- ‚úÖ **Zero property tax** ‚Äî no annual property tax (only service charges)
- 5% **VAT** on commercial property (residential is exempt)
- 4% **DLD transfer fee** on purchase (essentially a one-time "tax")

### Home Country Considerations (Israel)
- üî¥ **Israeli tax residents** are taxed on **worldwide income**
- üî¥ Rental income from Dubai property is taxable in Israel
- üî¥ Capital gains on foreign property sale are taxable
- üü° Foreign tax credits may be available (but UAE charges no tax, so no credit)
- üü° Tax treaty between Israel and UAE (signed 2021) may provide some relief
- üî¥ Reporting obligations: Must declare foreign property ownership and income to Israeli Tax Authority
- üî¥ Israeli capital gains tax: **25% on real capital gain** for foreign investment property
- üü° **Two rental income tax options (choose the lower one):**
  - **Option A ‚Äî 15% flat rate** (Section 122A, Israeli Tax Ordinance): No deductions allowed. Simple. Most small landlords choose this.
  - **Option B ‚Äî Marginal tax rate with deductions:** Can deduct building depreciation (2% of building value per year, excluding land), maintenance, management fees, insurance, etc. Better if deductions are high or other income is low.
- üü° **Building depreciation:** 2% of the building component per year (not land value). On a 500K AED property where ~80% is building: ~8,000 AED/year deductible under Option B.

### Estimated Israeli Tax Impact (Motor City ~500K, rent 56K/yr):

**Option A ‚Äî 15% Flat Rate (simpler, usually chosen):**

| Item | Gross Amount (AED) | Israeli Tax | Net After Tax |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual rent | 56,000 | 15% = ~8,400 | 47,600 |
| Capital gain (5yr, ~108K) | 108,000 | 25% = ~27,000 | 81,000 |

**Option B ‚Äî Marginal Rate with Deductions (example):**

| Item | Amount (AED) |
|---|---|
| Annual rent | 56,000 |
| Less: Depreciation (2% √ó 400K building) | (8,000) |
| Less: Service charges, maintenance | (8,500) |
| Taxable income | 39,500 |
| Tax at marginal rate (depends on brackets) | Varies |

> üí° **Most small landlords choose Option A (15% flat)** ‚Äî it's simpler and often lower unless you have very high deductible expenses or low marginal tax rates.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Consult a cross-border tax advisor** ‚Äî Israeli tax on foreign rental income and capital gains can significantly reduce returns. There may be optimization strategies (LLC structure, timing of gains, depreciation schedules).

## Key Financial Takeaways

1. **Expected 5-year annualized ROI: 8-9%** in the base case ‚Äî competitive globally
2. **Dubai is tax-free** but Israeli rental tax (15% flat) and capital gains tax (25%) will reduce returns by ~15-20%
3. **1% monthly payment plans** are excellent for cash flow management
4. **Budget AED 80K+ above property price** for DLD fees, furnishing, and initial costs
5. **Mortgage leverage** can significantly boost ROI but adds risk
6. **Vacancy is your biggest near-term risk** ‚Äî furnish attractively and price competitively
7. **The first 2 years** post-handover are critical ‚Äî establish rental history and tenant base
8. **Short-term rentals** can boost yields 30-50% but require more active management
